# 5. Best Practices for Tony's Window Cleaning

## Classes & Locations — skip them
QBO Classes/Locations add power for multi-crew or multi-branch companies. You
get service-line profitability from the income-account structure (doc 01) and
Sales-by-Product reports with zero extra clicking. Revisit Classes when you
run two crews.

## Tags — use exactly one group: Lead Source
You're spending on ads, so measure them. Create tag group **Lead Source** with
tags: `Facebook`, `Google`, `Referral`, `Repeat`, `Yard Sign`, `Nextdoor`.
Tag every invoice. Monthly, run Reports → Tags and compare revenue per source
to ad spend in 6000. That's your marketing ROI in one screen.

## Customer naming
Right now you have "Linda" and "Linda Smith" — collisions guaranteed. Convention:

- **Residential:** `Last, First – City` → `Smith, Linda – Apple Valley`
- **Commercial (coming):** `Business Name – City`, with the contact person in
  the customer details.
- Repeat address, different job? Same customer — never create "Linda 2".
- Recurring commercial locations can use **sub-customers** per site later.

## Estimates, not $0 invoices
Invoice 1048 went out at $0.00 as a way to show an itemized scope. That's what
**Estimates** are for: itemized, sendable, acceptable online, converts to an
invoice in one click when approved — and doesn't pollute your sales reports.
Quote → Estimate. Work done → convert → Invoice.

## Invoice & estimate template
Gear → Custom form styles:
- Logo + your site URL, consistent accent color.
- Payment instructions on every invoice; **enable ACH/bank transfer** in
  QuickBooks Payments and say "bank transfer preferred" — cards are costing
  you ~4.5%; ACH runs ~1%.
- Default terms: **Due on receipt** for residential; Net 15 for commercial.
- Footer: license/insured line + your lifetime-warranty language on screen
  re-mesh and bird mesh (it's a selling point — put it on the paper, not as a
  $0 line item).
- Estimate footer: "Valid 30 days. 20% deposit secures materials for
  pigeon-proofing jobs" (and the deposit gets invoiced with the Customer
  Deposit item — doc 02).

## Recurring transactions
Gear → Recurring transactions:
- Monthly **expense reminders** for: QuickBooks subscription, insurance
  premium, phone, any software — so a missed feed never loses a deduction.
- If you land recurring commercial or bin-cleaning routes: recurring invoices
  (monthly/quarterly) with auto-send — this is the single best growth
  habit for route work.

## Monthly reports (15 minutes, first Monday)
1. **Profit & Loss** — this month vs. last month, accrual.
2. **P&L year-to-date by month** — spot the trend line.
3. **Sales by Product/Service Summary** — your service-line scoreboard.
4. **A/R Aging Summary** — anything red, chase today.
5. **Bank reconciliation reports** — all business accounts show reconciled
   through last month.
6. Quarterly: revenue by Lead Source tag vs. 6000 ad spend.

## Habits that keep the file clean forever
- One business checking + one business card; **every** business dollar flows
  through them; **no** personal spending from them (Owner's Draw when it slips).
- Photo every receipt into QuickBooks mobile at the register — 10 seconds.
- Invoice same-day as the work; sales receipt same-day for cash jobs.
- Never "Add" a bank-feed deposit that's a customer payment — always **Match**.
- 25–30% of each month's net profit → tax reserve savings. Estimated taxes
  paid from it are recorded as Owner's Draws.
- When you hire your first employee: that week, set up payroll (QBO Payroll or
  Gusto), workers' comp, and add the payroll accounts from doc 01 — don't run
  wages through Zelle.
