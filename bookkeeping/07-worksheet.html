6

Retire the “Sales” category

~2 min

Two different things are both named “Sales”: the item (handled in Parts 4–5) and the income account (this step). Retire the account last:

1  Run Reports ▸ Profit & Loss, dates Jan 1 – today. Confirm the Sales line now reads $0.00 (Part 4 moved it into your service lines). If a little revenue lingers, it's from an old one-off item — do the optional invoice cleanup on the last page, or leave it as a small historical bucket.
2  Chart of accounts ▸ find Sales ▸ dropdown arrow ▸ Make inactive. QuickBooks may label this “Delete,” but it only deactivates — past reports keep their history as “Sales (deleted).”
3  Do the same for Billable Expense Income if it's empty. If it won't deactivate, go Gear ▸ Account and settings ▸ Expenses ▸ “Track billable expenses as income” ▸ switch its account to Materials & Reimbursements, then make it inactive.

8

How to bill every job from now on

keep by the desk
1 One line per service, standard items only. The item never changes — quantity, rate, and description do. The description is free text the customer sees; it doesn't affect the books.
2 Combos = multiple lines, never a bundle item. Windows + solar is two lines. Quoted one price? Adjust the line rates so they add up to it.
3 Deposits use “Customer Deposit Received,” both ways. +amount on the deposit invoice, −amount line on the final invoice. Never subtract a deposit in the Discount field.
4 Discounts go in the Discount field only — so you can see what price breaks cost you per year. Never a negative service line.
5 Free/courtesy work lives in a description, never a $0 line. A scope breakdown before the sale is an Estimate, not a $0 invoice.
6 Materials get their own line only on purpose — the Materials items, at cost. Otherwise price lump-sum and skip it.

The routine that keeps it clean

under 1 hr/wk
Every Monday · ~30 minLast day of month · ~30 min
Match each customer payment to its invoice — never “Add” a deposit, or income doubles.
Categorize the rest (Zelle to you = Draw; personal = Draw; between your accounts = Transfer).
Invoice last week's jobs; nudge anything 3+ days overdue.
Check: no account negative, Undeposited Funds empty, no negative A/R. 		Reconcile each business account to its statement.
Read the P&L, this month vs last.
Sales by Product/Service — your service scoreboard.
A/R Aging — call anyone 14+ days out.
Set aside 25–30% of net profit for taxes.
During the week: photograph every receipt into the QuickBooks mobile app at the register — ten seconds, and it saves you a deduction hunt later.
